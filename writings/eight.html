eight is just a chained zero

i.

come back home

where the sky is never really empty.  

my daddy said that

time moves faster because you have less of it

never understood what he meant until

every breath couldn’t be recreated

and i was left with salt licks

for eyes.

ii.

my heart and brain are two strangers

in a closed house.

iii.

look at me but look away

your eyes are stars and the dust falls upon your cheeks

like a splatter of embarrassment—

when you glimpse down at me, don’t you see that

i can only pray to a god i don’t believe in

so my knees look like

andromeda.

iv.

the harder i grasp

on to this dream world, the faster it slips

between my tongue

like how water rolls off his broken words.

v.

it looks like we made love

across the sky

so i wait until it bleeds out

and i’ll let you carve your name into my chest

if you promise

to come back and finish the job.

vi.

(but please leave the imprints of your fingers

along my personal canvas,

i don’t want to breathe unless

i can swallow yours.)

vii.

turn eight

180 degrees

and tell me what changed.

viii.

note to self:

buried under my fingertips

resides the remnants of our life

we were(n’t) supposed to—

and i thought you knew that

a pretty chance like this

doesn’t come when you call for it

(say something, say anything, say)

split my heart in two

wrap it in a handkerchief

for your breast pocket

to keep safe.

make me your home

so i can look at myself

through the blacks of your eyes

and bathe in the contentment.
