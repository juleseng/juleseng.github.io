tsunami;crocodile tears

stoplights in tandem, obsidian sky

my fatal flaw was that i could never run a yellow.

lurched like a dead man and when you see it

don’t scream

‘cause if the everglades were real

i’d be dead by tomorrow.

 

what’s left after the end of the end?

my parents, brittle like speckled clay.

disintegrated. centrifuge. and gone like the wind on a wet tuesday,

left blood on my limp hands.

 

sap in my ear; muffled, cruel notions

turned the war in my heart cold.

i reach my pinky out, my mantra croaks.

promises, they’re meant to be swallowed whole.
